01 — Sassoon Dock
Same fish.
Different systems.

Most people think design is about what you see. It's not. It's about how consistently something performs — across every place it shows up.

I went to Sassoon Dock early one morning. Not as an observer. As a designer trying to understand systems in the real world. Amid the noise, speed, and chaos — there was a pattern. Not in the fish. In the decisions.

And the clearest understanding didn't come from the buyers. It came from the women sitting on the ground — sorting the catch, watching every transaction, every day. They see everything.

And they told me something simple. There are two types of buyers here — and they revealed everything I needed to understand about design.

02 — The Two Buyers

Two buyers. Two definitions of quality.

01 / 02

The Premium Buyer

They don't rush. They're not looking at "fish." They're looking at uniformity. Same size. Same cut. No variation. Because this isn't a one-time purchase — it feeds into a larger system.

Export lines. Restaurant chains. Scaled kitchens. If one piece is off, the system breaks.

So what they're really buying is not the product.

Consistency Control Predictability
02 / 02

The Local Buyer

Completely different rhythm. Fast. Direct. "How much?" "Best price?" They don't need perfection — because their world runs differently.

Daily sales. Tight margins. Constant movement. They're not building systems. They're sustaining momentum.

So what they're buying is not uniformity.

Value Speed Momentum
03 — My Approach

Now replace
fish with design.

Some businesses need cohesion. A system that holds across everything — packaging, website, decks, physical spaces. Where every touchpoint feels connected.

Others need execution. A clear output. Delivered fast. Solving an immediate need.

The mistake? Treating both the same. That's where design starts to fail.

Because design is not the deliverable. It's the response to a specific gap.

I don't start with a logo. Or a website. Or a brochure.
I start with one question: What is the gap we're solving?
01 Define the gap. The distance between where your brand is — and where it needs to perform.
02 Choose the response. If the gap is consistency, we build a system. If the gap is visibility, we move with speed.
03 Same tools. Different intent. Because if we don't define the gap — we won't build a brand. We'll just produce work.
